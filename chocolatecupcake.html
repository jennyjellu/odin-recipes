Dairy Free Cupcakes

The best dairy-free cupcakes you'll ever make.

Rich, moist, and intensely fudgy chocolate cupcakes made entirely without milk, butter, or sour cream. By using oil for an incredibly soft crumb and hot liquid to "bloom" the cocoa powder, this foolproof recipe delivers bakery-quality depth and a perfectly airy texture.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup of butter
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 cup warm brewed coffee
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup unsweetwned cocoa powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder

Recipe

  1. Preheat your oven and line a 12-cup muffin tin with paper liners.
  2. Combine the flour, sugar, cocoa powder, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Whisk well to break up any cocoa clumps and incorporate air.
  3. Add the egg, oil, and vanilla extract to the bowl. Beat on medium speed or whisk vigorously for about a minute until the mixture is uniform. It will look quite thick at this stage.
  4. Turn your mixer to low (or whisk gently) and slowly pour in the hot water or coffee. Mix until just combined. Don't panic: the batter will be very thin and watery, but that's exactly what gives them their soft texture.
  5. Divide the batter evenly among the 12 liners, filling each about two-thirds full. Bake until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean. Let them cool in the pan for 5 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.
